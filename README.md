# Renewable Energy Analytics Dashboard in Power BI

This project analyses renewable energy development in Australia from 2005 to 2023 using Power BI. It explores how Australia’s renewable electricity share has changed over time, how the renewable energy mix has shifted, how Australia compares with selected regional countries, and how renewable generation may evolve toward 2030.

The project demonstrates business intelligence, dashboard design, data cleaning, forecasting, energy analytics and data storytelling.

---

## Project Overview

Renewable energy has become central to Australia’s long-term energy security, sustainability and emissions reduction strategy. This project uses public energy datasets to evaluate Australia’s progress in renewable electricity generation and to communicate the results through interactive Power BI dashboards.

The analysis focuses on four main business questions:

- How has Australia’s renewable energy share changed since 2005?
- How has the composition of renewable energy sources changed?
- How does Australia compare with Japan, Indonesia and New Zealand?
- What does the forecast suggest for renewable energy generation by 2030?

---

## Objectives

- Analyse Australia’s renewable electricity generation trends from 2005 to 2023
- Compare renewable and non-renewable energy source contributions
- Identify growth patterns in solar, wind, hydro and bioenergy
- Benchmark Australia’s renewable generation against regional peers
- Forecast renewable generation to 2030 using Power BI forecasting
- Present insights through clear dashboard visuals and written reporting

---

## Dataset

The project uses publicly available energy datasets, including:

- Australian Energy Statistics data
- International renewable electricity generation data for Australia, Japan, Indonesia and New Zealand

The main fields used include:

- Year
- Country
- Energy source / technology
- Electricity generation in GWh
- Renewable energy category
- Total electricity generation
- Renewable proportion

---

## Methods

The workflow included:

- Importing energy datasets into Power BI
- Cleaning and transforming tables in Power Query
- Standardising year, country, technology and generation fields
- Creating calculated fields such as renewable proportion
- Appending country-level datasets for regional comparison
- Building dashboard visuals for trends, source composition and comparison
- Applying Power BI forecasting to project renewable generation to 2030
- Writing a supporting analytical report explaining the insights

---

## Key Findings

- Australia’s renewable electricity share increased from around 9% in 2005 to over 33% by 2023.
- Solar and wind became the major drivers of renewable generation growth.
- Hydro remained relatively stable but declined as a proportion of the renewable mix.
- Australia showed strong renewable growth after 2015, supported by policy incentives and falling technology costs.
- Japan remained ahead in absolute renewable generation, but Australia showed strong growth momentum.
- Power BI forecasting suggested continued renewable generation growth toward 2030.
