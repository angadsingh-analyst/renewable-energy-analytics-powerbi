# Renewable Energy Analytics Dashboard in Power BI

## Project Overview

This project analyses Australia's renewable energy transition using Power BI. The dashboard explores changes in renewable electricity generation, shifts in the renewable energy mix, regional comparisons with selected Asia-Pacific countries, and a forecast of Australian renewable generation through 2030.

The project demonstrates business intelligence, data cleaning, dashboard design, time-series analysis, comparative analytics and forecasting.

## Analytical Questions

1. How has the proportion of renewable energy in Australia's electricity generation changed since 2005?
2. How has the composition of renewable energy sources changed over time?
3. How does Australia's renewable electricity generation compare with Japan, Indonesia and New Zealand?
4. What does the forecast suggest for Australia's renewable energy generation by 2030?

## Key Findings

- Australia's renewable electricity share increased from approximately 9% in 2005 to over 33% by 2023.
- Growth was driven mainly by wind and solar PV, while hydro remained relatively stable.
- Australia showed strong post-2015 growth compared with selected regional peers, although Japan remained larger in total renewable generation.
- The forecast suggests continued growth toward 2030, with renewable generation potentially approaching approximately 190K GWh under trend-based assumptions.
- Additional investment in grid infrastructure, battery energy storage, transmission networks and policy support is needed to sustain the projected transition.
